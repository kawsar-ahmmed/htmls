For Marketing Team — How our system tracks user actions & what we send to Meta
🏢 Platform Layer 🛒 Store Layer 🛡️ Fraud Layer 📊 15 Meta Events
The 3 tracking layers
🏢
Platform Layer
Platform DB — 25+ events
Tracks business-level activity. Who signed up, paid, set up their store, and what plan they're on. This is the "who are they and are they paying?" layer.
Signups Subscriptions Payments Domains Trial
🛒
Store Layer
Store DB — 20+ events
Tracks what happens inside the seller's store. Products, orders, deliveries, courier updates. This is the "what's happening in their business?" layer.
Products Orders Deliveries Couriers
🛡️
Fraud Layer
Fraud DB — 15+ events
Scores every order for risk and blocks bad actors. This layer is the gatekeeper — it decides if a Purchase event is allowed to reach Meta or not.
Risk scoring Blocking Suppression

🛡️ Why the Fraud Layer matters for Meta
Clean order → Fraud check passes → Purchase event fires to Meta → Meta optimizes toward real buyers
Flagged order → Fraud check fails → Purchase event is silently blocked → Meta never sees it → Ad quality stays clean

Trigger View Events — What screen did they see?

These fire automatically in the browser the moment a specific screen loads. They let Meta know what emotional/motivational frame the user was shown.

→
loss_reminder_shown → Meta

→
missed_value_shown → Meta

→
saved_value_reminder_shown → Meta
